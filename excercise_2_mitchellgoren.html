The Origins of Pawnbroking

Our Services

Consumers usually utilize short-term, collateralized pawn shop loans. As collateral, the borrower pledges a possession, typically $75-$100.5, in exchange for the loan. A pledge is forfeited when the borrower fails to repay the principal plus interest by the maturity date (typically 30 to 90 days) and ultimately resold.
